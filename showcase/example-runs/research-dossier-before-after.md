# Research Dossier: Before / After

## Weak output

The weak version reads like broad synthesis without decision support:

- "The market is evolving quickly."
- "There are several promising vendors and approaches."
- "More research may be needed before committing."

That language sounds safe and leaves leadership with no clearer decision posture than before.

## Stronger target output

### 1. Decision frame

- The real question is not whether the category is interesting.
- The question is whether the team should pilot now, monitor for another quarter, or avoid the space entirely until one dependency is resolved.

### 2. Evidence structure

- Strong evidence: repeated operator pain appears across interviews and win-loss notes.
- Mixed evidence: vendor claims about deployment speed are consistent in marketing but inconsistent in implementation notes.
- Thin evidence: ROI claims rely heavily on vendor-authored case studies.

### 3. Recommendation posture

1. Pilot with a narrow workflow where operator pain is already visible.
2. Do not treat ROI claims as decision-grade until customer-verified implementation data is collected.
3. Revisit the broader rollout decision only after the pilot answers two unresolved implementation questions.

### 4. Explicit uncertainty

- The current signal is strong enough for a scoped pilot.
- It is not strong enough for category-wide positioning or a broad roadmap commitment.

## Why the stronger version matters

Good research synthesis narrows the next move. It distinguishes what the evidence supports now from what would still be guesswork.
