# Customer Interview Synthesizer: Before / After

## Weak output

The weak version produces familiar but low-value insight wallpaper:

- "Users want better visibility."
- "People care about ease of use."
- "There may be an opportunity to improve onboarding."

That output compresses every interview into bland agreement and destroys the evidence hierarchy.

## Stronger target output

### 1. Recurring themes with segment split

- Mid-market admins repeatedly described setup friction around permissions and data imports.
- Enterprise operators cared less about setup and more about auditability, approval flow, and rollback confidence.

### 2. Important tension, not false consensus

- Smaller teams wanted fewer controls and faster time to value.
- Larger teams wanted more explicit governance, even if it slowed initial adoption.
- Those are not the same "ease of use" request and should not drive one undifferentiated recommendation.

### 3. Evidence-weighted implications

1. Prioritize setup-path clarity for the mid-market onboarding motion.
2. Treat governance and auditability as expansion blockers for larger accounts, not onboarding polish.
3. Validate whether the operator pain is concentrated in one implementation segment before broad roadmap changes.

### 4. What still needs validation

- The sample is skewed toward recent implementations.
- The strongest governance feedback came from accounts with custom approval flows.
- The next interview round should include lower-touch customers to test whether the pattern generalizes.

## Why the stronger version matters

The stronger version protects the team from fake consensus. It keeps segment differences visible, marks the evidence weight honestly, and turns interviews into better product decisions instead of softer summaries.
