# Opportunity Solution Tree

## Summary

Structure an opportunity solution tree from product goals, opportunities, and candidate solutions.

## When To Use

- The team wants to connect a product goal to candidate discovery paths.
- You need an opportunity solution tree with goal alignment, opportunity branches, solution options, and assumption notes instead of unstructured notes.
- The workflow repeats often enough to justify a reusable asset.

## Avoid When

- The team has not defined the product area or user segment at all yet.
- You need direct user validation rather than a synthesis artifact.

## Inputs

- goal
- known opportunities
- candidate ideas
- constraints

## Output Contract

- opportunity tree
- assumption notes
- discovery next steps

## Workflow

1. Confirm the decision context, audience, and missing inputs.
2. Build an opportunity solution tree with goal alignment, opportunity branches, solution options, and assumption notes using the available evidence and constraints.
3. Separate facts from assumptions and make confidence explicit.
4. Finish with next actions, open questions, or escalation notes.

## Deliverable Shape

An opportunity solution tree with goal alignment, opportunity branches, solution options, and assumption notes

## Guardrails

- Do not invent facts that are not present in the supplied materials.
- Make uncertainty explicit when inputs are incomplete or contradictory.
- Keep the output structured enough that another operator can reuse it.

## Maturity Note

This Skill is part of the seeded breadth layer. It is intentionally lighter than the developed and flagship tiers.
