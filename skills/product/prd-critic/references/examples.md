# Examples

## Bloated scope review

### Scenario

A PRD bundles multiple feature ideas together and the team needs a critique before engineering sizing begins.

### Prompt

Review this PRD for scope bloat, hidden assumptions, and readiness for implementation.

### Strong Response Should

- Identify where the document mixes separate bets.
- Show which requirements are vague or untestable.
- Recommend the highest-leverage revisions.

## Leadership alignment

### Scenario

A product lead needs to send the PRD to leadership but wants to catch weak logic before that meeting.

### Prompt

Critique the PRD with a focus on decision quality, tradeoffs, and risk communication.

### Strong Response Should

- Separate product logic from presentation polish.
- Highlight decisions leadership still needs to make.
- Explain what evidence is missing.

## Cross-functional handoff

### Scenario

Design and engineering are about to consume the PRD and need to know where ambiguity will hurt downstream work.

### Prompt

Analyze the PRD for handoff risk, unresolved dependencies, and missing success criteria.

### Strong Response Should

- Call out ambiguous requirements.
- Identify dependencies and missing stakeholders.
- End with a revision sequence the PM should tackle first.
