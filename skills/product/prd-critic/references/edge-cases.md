# Edge Cases

## Metrics sound precise but prove little

### Risk

The PRD includes numeric targets, but the numbers do not establish causality or a meaningful user outcome.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Question the metric logic directly, distinguish outcome metrics from activity metrics, and flag where the document is mistaking instrumentation for proof.

### Failure Probe

Treats quantified goals as inherently rigorous and misses that they do not validate the product bet.

## Hidden dependency load

### Risk

The document appears self-contained but relies on go-to-market, legal, data, or platform work that is barely acknowledged.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Surface the dependency map, classify which dependencies are gating, and show how the plan changes if one of them slips.

### Failure Probe

Reviews only the feature surface and ignores the operational work required to ship it.

## Executive pressure to approve quickly

### Risk

Stakeholders want the PRD approved this week even though the evidence and acceptance criteria remain soft.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Separate what is ready for commitment from what still needs decision or discovery work, and make the risk of premature approval explicit.

### Failure Probe

Softens the critique into stylistic comments because the timeline feels politically sensitive.
