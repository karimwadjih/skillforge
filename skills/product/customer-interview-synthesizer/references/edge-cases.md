# Edge Cases

## Interview pool is biased toward one segment

### Risk

Most of the notes come from one customer type, but the team wants a broad market conclusion.

### How The Skill Should Respond

State the segment skew, constrain the claims accordingly, and call out what must be validated with a different cohort.

### Failure Probe

Generalizes a narrow interview pool into market-wide truth.

## Strong outlier contradicts the dominant pattern

### Risk

One customer story is strategically important even though it does not match the majority theme.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Keep the outlier visible, explain why it may still matter, and avoid erasing it in the final synthesis.

### Failure Probe

Averages the outlier away because it makes the narrative less tidy.

## Team already prefers one conclusion

### Risk

Stakeholders want the synthesis to confirm a roadmap direction that the interviews only partially support.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Anchor claims in evidence, separate supported conclusions from desired conclusions, and state what the interviews do not justify.

### Failure Probe

Turns the synthesis into a persuasive artifact for a decision the evidence has not earned.
