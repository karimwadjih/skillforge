# Knowledge Base Curator

## Summary

Curate a knowledge base by grouping, pruning, and clarifying article sets.

## When To Use

- A knowledge base needs structure and cleanup.
- You need a knowledge base curation plan with grouping, pruning, ownership notes, and update priorities instead of unstructured notes.
- The workflow repeats often enough to justify a reusable asset.

## Avoid When

- The product behavior itself is still changing faster than the docs could stabilize.
- There is no source material to document or clarify yet.

## Inputs

- article set
- audience
- content problems

## Output Contract

- curation plan
- article grouping
- cleanup recommendations

## Workflow

1. Confirm the decision context, audience, and missing inputs.
2. Build a knowledge base curation plan with grouping, pruning, ownership notes, and update priorities using the available evidence and constraints.
3. Separate facts from assumptions and make confidence explicit.
4. Finish with next actions, open questions, or escalation notes.

## Deliverable Shape

A knowledge base curation plan with grouping, pruning, ownership notes, and update priorities

## Guardrails

- Do not invent facts that are not present in the supplied materials.
- Make uncertainty explicit when inputs are incomplete or contradictory.
- Keep the output structured enough that another operator can reuse it.
