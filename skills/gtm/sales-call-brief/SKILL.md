# Sales Call Brief

## Summary

Prepare an account-specific call brief with stakeholder intent, likely objections, and the best next move.

## When To Use

- A high-stakes prospect, renewal, or escalation call needs sharper prep than the CRM notes provide.
- You need a brief with account context, stakeholder goals, likely objections, proof priorities, and follow-up ownership instead of scattered notes.
- The organization repeats this pre-call work enough that the posture should be reusable.

## Avoid When

- You have almost no account context and need discovery questions more than a brief.
- The meeting is low stakes and does not justify a custom prep artifact.

## Inputs

- account context and stage
- recent activity or call notes
- meeting objective
- known stakeholders and risks
- available proof points or weak spots

## Output Contract

- sales call brief
- stakeholder-specific talk track priorities
- objection prep
- follow-up plan

## Workflow

1. Confirm the decision context, audience, and missing inputs.
2. Build a sales call brief with account context, stakeholder goals, objection handling posture, proof priorities, and follow-up ownership using the available evidence and constraints.
3. Separate facts from assumptions and make confidence explicit.
4. Finish with next actions, open questions, or escalation notes.

## Deliverable Shape

A sales call brief with account context, stakeholder goals, objection handling posture, proof priorities, and follow-up ownership

## Guardrails

- Do not invent facts that are not present in the supplied materials.
- Make uncertainty explicit when inputs are incomplete or contradictory.
- Keep the output structured enough that another operator can reuse it.
