# Edge Cases

## CRM data is stale or contradictory

### Risk

The record includes outdated notes, mixed signals, or inconsistent stage reporting.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Flag stale context, separate high-confidence facts from softer signals, and avoid overfitting the brief to bad CRM hygiene.

### Failure Probe

The response ignores the complication or hides the uncertainty instead of adapting the workflow.

## Stakeholders have conflicting incentives

### Risk

The economic buyer, operator, and security reviewer want different things from the call.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Keep the stakeholder goals distinct and avoid flattening the conversation into one generic talk track.

### Failure Probe

The response ignores the complication or hides the uncertainty instead of adapting the workflow.

## Proof is weaker than the team wants

### Risk

The account team wants to lean on claims or references that are not actually strong enough for the objection at hand.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Downgrade weak proof, suggest safer supporting evidence, and keep the brief from promising what the team cannot substantiate live.

### Failure Probe

The response ignores the complication or hides the uncertainty instead of adapting the workflow.
