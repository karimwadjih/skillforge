# Examples

## Renewal rescue call

### Scenario

A large renewal is at risk because adoption is uneven and an executive sponsor is questioning expansion value.

### Prompt

Prepare a renewal rescue call brief that sharpens the customer context, likely objections, and the strongest conversation path for the account team.

### Strong Response Should

- Frame the real deal risk instead of just summarizing the account record.
- Anticipate executive and operator objections separately.
- Recommend the next-best conversation move rather than a generic pitch sequence.

## Security review call

### Scenario

A late-stage deal hinges on a security and procurement conversation with both technical and business stakeholders present.

### Prompt

Create a sales call brief for a security review meeting with likely objections, proof priorities, and follow-up ownership.

### Strong Response Should

- Separate stakeholder goals instead of treating the audience as one buyer.
- Match proof points to the specific objections likely to surface.
- Leave the team with a practical follow-up plan if the meeting stalls.

## Expansion call with skeptical operator

### Scenario

An expansion opportunity depends on an operator who likes the product but distrusts the implementation burden and internal change cost.

### Prompt

Build a call brief for the expansion meeting that accounts for operator skepticism, executive upside, and the most credible proof sequence.

### Strong Response Should

- Treat implementation burden as a central objection, not a side note.
- Balance executive value framing with operator-level credibility.
- Recommend a conversation move that earns the next step instead of overselling the expansion.
