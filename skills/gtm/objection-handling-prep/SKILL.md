# Objection Handling Prep

## Summary

Prepare objection handling guidance for sales, partnerships, or launch support teams.

## When To Use

- A go-to-market team wants a better objection handling asset.
- You need an objection handling guide with concern framing, response options, and proof guidance instead of unstructured notes.
- The workflow repeats often enough to justify a reusable asset.

## Avoid When

- There is no defined audience, channel, or launch moment yet.
- You need final legal review or brand approval rather than a working draft.

## Inputs

- objection list
- product context
- proof points
- known constraints

## Output Contract

- objection guide
- response options
- evidence notes

## Workflow

1. Confirm the decision context, audience, and missing inputs.
2. Build an objection handling guide with concern framing, response options, and proof guidance using the available evidence and constraints.
3. Separate facts from assumptions and make confidence explicit.
4. Finish with next actions, open questions, or escalation notes.

## Deliverable Shape

An objection handling guide with concern framing, response options, and proof guidance

## Guardrails

- Do not invent facts that are not present in the supplied materials.
- Make uncertainty explicit when inputs are incomplete or contradictory.
- Keep the output structured enough that another operator can reuse it.
