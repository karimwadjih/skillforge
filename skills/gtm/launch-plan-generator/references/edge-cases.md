# Edge Cases

## Launch date is fixed but dependencies are not

### Risk

The team has a public date committed while several gating decisions or workstreams remain unstable.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Make the hard gates explicit, show contingency branches, and force visibility into what would trigger a scope cut or delay.

### Failure Probe

Produces a confident timeline that assumes every dependency resolves on schedule.

## One cross-functional team is the bottleneck

### Risk

Legal, analytics, support, or enablement work is likely to constrain the entire launch.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Center the bottleneck in the plan, identify parallelizable work around it, and clarify what can and cannot proceed without it.

### Failure Probe

Treats all workstreams as equally paced and hides the actual blocking team.

## Post-launch support load is uncertain

### Risk

The team expects adoption but is unclear on likely ticket volume, incident risk, or watch metrics.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Define the immediate operating posture, monitoring set, and fallback actions rather than pretending launch is routine.

### Failure Probe

Ships a plan that ends at announcement copy and says nothing about launch-week operations.
