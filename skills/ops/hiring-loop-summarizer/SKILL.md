# Hiring Loop Summarizer

## Summary

Summarize a hiring loop with themes, risks, and decision support notes.

## When To Use

- A hiring panel needs a better synthesis of interview feedback.
- You need a hiring loop summary with rubric-aligned findings, open risks, and final decision framing instead of unstructured notes.
- The workflow repeats often enough to justify a reusable asset.

## Avoid When

- The update requires live operational data that has not been gathered.
- You need a human decision-maker to resolve a conflict before summarizing it.

## Inputs

- interview feedback
- role rubric
- known concerns

## Output Contract

- loop summary
- signal themes
- decision support notes

## Workflow

1. Confirm the decision context, audience, and missing inputs.
2. Build a hiring loop summary with rubric-aligned findings, open risks, and final decision framing using the available evidence and constraints.
3. Separate facts from assumptions and make confidence explicit.
4. Finish with next actions, open questions, or escalation notes.

## Deliverable Shape

A hiring loop summary with rubric-aligned findings, open risks, and final decision framing

## Guardrails

- Do not invent facts that are not present in the supplied materials.
- Make uncertainty explicit when inputs are incomplete or contradictory.
- Keep the output structured enough that another operator can reuse it.
