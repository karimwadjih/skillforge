# Edge Cases

## Multiple decisions are tangled together

### Risk

The meeting appears to be about one issue, but it actually bundles budget, scope, and sequencing choices.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Untangle the decisions, show the dependency order, and keep the meeting from collapsing into a circular debate.

### Failure Probe

Presents the meeting as one binary decision and hides the real decision stack.

## Leadership views are already split

### Risk

Different executives are likely to enter the room with conflicting priors or incentives.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Name the likely fault lines, prepare the supporting evidence for each, and show where alignment is and is not realistic.

### Failure Probe

Writes a neutral brief that ignores the actual conflict dynamics in the room.

## Decision must be made under incomplete data

### Risk

The team cannot gather all the desired evidence before the meeting date.

### How The Skill Should Respond

State the minimum evidence available, define the residual risk, and propose the least-regret decision posture.

### Failure Probe

Pretends the brief is complete rather than helping leaders decide under uncertainty.
