# Examples

## Founder-led startup scaling up

### Scenario

A startup is moving from founder-driven coordination to a functional leadership team and wants fewer surprises without adding bureaucracy for its own sake.

### Prompt

Design an operating rhythm for a scaling startup with weekly execution reviews, monthly planning, and explicit escalation handling across product, engineering, and GTM.

### Strong Response Should

- Define which decisions belong in which cadence rather than stacking more meetings.
- Specify the artifact each forum requires to stay useful.
- Make escalation paths explicit when a decision cannot wait for the next scheduled review.

## Distributed engineering and product cadence

### Scenario

A distributed organization has metrics arriving late, duplicated reviews, and recurring disagreement about where prioritization decisions should happen.

### Prompt

Create an operating rhythm that reduces duplicated forums, clarifies decision ownership, and matches meeting cadence to data freshness.

### Strong Response Should

- Match cadence to how quickly meaningful signal actually arrives.
- Collapse redundant forums instead of renaming them.
- Clarify who decides, who prepares the artifact, and when escalation is triggered.

## Post-incident operating reset

### Scenario

After several avoidable escalations, leadership wants to redesign the weekly operating system so risks surface earlier and owners do not improvise under pressure.

### Prompt

Design an operating rhythm that improves weekly signal review, issue escalation, and owner discipline after repeated cross-functional misses.

### Strong Response Should

- Tie the rhythm to the specific operating failures that keep recurring.
- Define what gets surfaced proactively versus what waits for escalation.
- Avoid creating a ceremony-heavy model that nobody can sustain.
