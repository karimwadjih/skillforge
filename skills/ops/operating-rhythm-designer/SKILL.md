# Operating Rhythm Designer

## Summary

Design an operating rhythm with clear decision cadence, artifact discipline, and escalation paths.

## When To Use

- The team has too many meetings, too little decision clarity, or too much status noise between leaders.
- You need a rhythm with cadence, artifacts, owners, and escalation paths instead of another calendar cleanup exercise.
- The operating problem repeats often enough that the coordination model itself should be reusable.

## Avoid When

- The organization is too early to define recurring decisions or owners at all.
- The actual problem is leadership conflict or missing authority, not cadence design.

## Inputs

- org shape and key functions
- recurring decisions that need cadence support
- metrics and reporting availability
- existing meetings or rituals
- known escalation failures

## Output Contract

- operating rhythm design
- meeting and artifact cadence map
- owner assignments
- escalation rules

## Workflow

1. Confirm the decision context, audience, and missing inputs.
2. Build an operating rhythm with decision cadence, recurring artifacts, meeting ownership, and escalation paths using the available evidence and constraints.
3. Separate facts from assumptions and make confidence explicit.
4. Finish with next actions, open questions, or escalation notes.

## Deliverable Shape

An operating rhythm with decision cadence, recurring artifacts, meeting ownership, and escalation paths

## Guardrails

- Do not invent facts that are not present in the supplied materials.
- Make uncertainty explicit when inputs are incomplete or contradictory.
- Keep the output structured enough that another operator can reuse it.
