# Edge Cases

## The week had lots of activity but little true movement

### Risk

Teams want their work represented, but the update should not inflate noise into signal.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Acknowledge the low-change week, summarize only the material movement, and keep the update short rather than padding it.

### Failure Probe

Manufactures significance so the update looks busy.

## One issue dominates the week

### Risk

A major incident or dependency problem crowds out every other topic.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Lead with the dominant issue, summarize the rest tersely, and avoid burying the real operating story under equal-weight bullets.

### Failure Probe

Gives every update item the same weight and obscures the main risk.

## Inputs from teams conflict

### Risk

Different owners describe the same operational issue with different severity or confidence.

### How The Skill Should Respond

State the discrepancy, note which interpretation is better supported, and flag the follow-up needed to reconcile it.

### Failure Probe

Combines conflicting status reports into a falsely coherent update.
