# Edge Cases

## Announcement is stronger than the underlying product

### Risk

The launch narrative is polished, but the actual capability appears thin, early, or hard to verify.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Separate messaging strength from product substance and lower confidence until durable evidence appears.

### Failure Probe

The response ignores the complication or hides the uncertainty instead of adapting the workflow.

## The competitor is not really targeting the same segment

### Risk

Teams overreact because the company name is familiar even though the move primarily targets a different buyer, deployment model, or market tier.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Re-center the analysis on actual segment overlap and show where the threat is real, adjacent, or mostly irrelevant.

### Failure Probe

The response ignores the complication or hides the uncertainty instead of adapting the workflow.

## Internal teams want a mirror response

### Risk

A visible competitor move creates pressure to match features or messaging even when the strategic logic is weak.

### How The Skill Should Respond

Make the case for restraint explicit and compare the cost of reaction against the evidence of actual market impact.

### Failure Probe

The response ignores the complication or hides the uncertainty instead of adapting the workflow.
