# Competitor Briefing

Turn competitor noise into a briefing about intent, durability, and what your team should ignore.

## Quick View

- Category: `research`
- Maturity: `experimental`
- Tier: `developed`
- Evaluation status: `sampled`

## What This Skill Does

Competitor analysis often swings between shallow feature comparison and mirror-response panic. Teams need a way to judge what changed, how durable the signal is, and whether any response is actually warranted.

## When To Reach For It

- A competitor announcement, launch, or pricing move is creating internal panic or overreaction.
- Leadership needs a brief that separates credible signal from theater before changing roadmap or GTM plans.

## What You Should Provide

- competitor signal set such as launches, pricing changes, win-loss notes, or field reports
- your current product or GTM context
- known customer or segment overlap
- decision horizon for response

## What You Should Get Back

- competitor briefing
- signal-confidence notes
- likely strategic intent
- recommended internal response options
