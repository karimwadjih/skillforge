# Source Credibility Audit

## Summary

Audit a set of sources for credibility, bias, recency, and usefulness.

## When To Use

- The research set includes sources with mixed credibility.
- You need a credibility audit that tiers sources and explains how each should or should not influence a decision instead of unstructured notes.
- The workflow repeats often enough to justify a reusable asset.

## Avoid When

- You need original field research rather than synthesis from available materials.
- The decision depends on live data that has not been collected yet.

## Inputs

- source list
- decision context
- quality concerns

## Output Contract

- credibility audit
- source tiers
- usage notes

## Workflow

1. Confirm the decision context, audience, and missing inputs.
2. Build a credibility audit that tiers sources and explains how each should or should not influence a decision using the available evidence and constraints.
3. Separate facts from assumptions and make confidence explicit.
4. Finish with next actions, open questions, or escalation notes.

## Deliverable Shape

A credibility audit that tiers sources and explains how each should or should not influence a decision

## Guardrails

- Do not invent facts that are not present in the supplied materials.
- Make uncertainty explicit when inputs are incomplete or contradictory.
- Keep the output structured enough that another operator can reuse it.
