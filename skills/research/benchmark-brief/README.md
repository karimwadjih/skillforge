# Benchmark Brief

Create a concise benchmark brief that explains what is being measured, how it is scored, and how to interpret the results.

## Quick View

- Category: `research`
- Maturity: `frontier`
- Tier: `flagship`
- Evaluation status: `benchmarked`

## What This Skill Does

Benchmark artifacts often contain raw scores without enough methodology or interpretation to make the results trustworthy.

## Why This Skill Matters

Benchmarks lose trust when they present numbers without clarifying what changed, what stayed constant, and what the benchmark still cannot say. This skill matters when evaluation needs to inform decisions instead of decorate a slide.

## What Makes It Different

- Explains benchmark intent and limitations before interpreting scores.
- Translates version movement into decision meaning rather than score theater.
- Makes caveats legible to non-specialists without dumbing down the method.

## When To Reach For It

- You need to design or explain an evaluation benchmark for an agent workflow.
- Stakeholders are asking for benchmark results without understanding what the numbers mean.

## What You Should Provide

- benchmark goal
- cases or prompts
- scoring dimensions
- known limitations

## What You Should Get Back

- benchmark brief
- measurement summary
- interpretation notes
- follow-up recommendations
