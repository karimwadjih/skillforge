# Rubric

| Criterion | Strong | Weak |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Debate structure | Shows the main schools of thought, where they overlap, and the specific fault lines that matter to the reader. | Produces a long source list that still leaves the debate shapeless. |
| Evidence discrimination | Separates seminal, credible, derivative, and speculative sources instead of flattening them into one reading pile. | Treats every citation as equally informative or equally current. |
| Reading leverage | Leaves the reader knowing what to read next, what to skip for now, and what question each source will clarify. | Adds reading volume without improving orientation or decision quality. |

## Certification Note

Certified status requires benchmark coverage, score threshold, and `manual_review: passed`. It is not granted by structure alone.
