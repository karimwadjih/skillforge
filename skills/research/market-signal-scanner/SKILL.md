# Market Signal Scanner

## Summary

Collect and rank external market signals for a team that needs a fast situational read.

## When To Use

- A market is changing and the team needs a concise weekly or monthly view.
- You need a ranked signal scanner output with significance, confidence, and monitoring recommendations instead of unstructured notes.
- The workflow repeats often enough to justify a reusable asset.

## Avoid When

- You need original field research rather than synthesis from available materials.
- The decision depends on live data that has not been collected yet.

## Inputs

- signal list
- industry context
- time window

## Output Contract

- ranked signal summary
- implication notes
- watch list

## Workflow

1. Confirm the decision context, audience, and missing inputs.
2. Build a ranked signal scanner output with significance, confidence, and monitoring recommendations using the available evidence and constraints.
3. Separate facts from assumptions and make confidence explicit.
4. Finish with next actions, open questions, or escalation notes.

## Deliverable Shape

A ranked signal scanner output with significance, confidence, and monitoring recommendations

## Guardrails

- Do not invent facts that are not present in the supplied materials.
- Make uncertainty explicit when inputs are incomplete or contradictory.
- Keep the output structured enough that another operator can reuse it.
